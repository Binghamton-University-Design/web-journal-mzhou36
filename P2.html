Project 2: Exquisite corpse

Overview

The exquisite corpse game, as developed by the Surrealists André Breton, Yves Tanguy, Jacques Prévert, and Marcel Duchamp during a hangout session in 1925, is an example of an emergent design pattern. With the exquisite corpse, participants develop a set of rules that can be followed in order to collaboratively create a work of art that embraces the unknown. For the Surrealists, unlocking psychic space was paramount. And as they discovered, one way in was through games that created an opening into the unknown.
